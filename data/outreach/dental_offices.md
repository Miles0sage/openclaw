## Key Pain Points to Hit in Conversation

1. **Phone overwhelm during peak hours:** "Your front desk is checking in patients, verifying insurance, handling paperwork — and the phone is ringing on top of all that. Something has to give. Usually it's the phone."
2. **After-hours missed calls:** "Patients don't just call 9-5. They Google you at night, on weekends. If they call and get voicemail, a lot of them will just call the next dentist on the list. The AI answers every single time."
3. **New patient acquisition cost:** "You're probably spending money on Google Ads or mailers to get people to call. If those calls go unanswered, you're paying for leads and then losing them at the last step."
4. **Routine question overload:** "How many calls a day are just 'what are your hours' or 'do you take my insurance'? The AI handles all of that instantly, and your front desk gets that time back."
5. **Scheduling complexity:** "Patients want to reschedule, cancel, or book follow-ups. Every one of those calls takes 3-5 minutes of staff time. The AI can handle the straightforward ones."
6. **Patient experience:** "Nobody likes being put on hold. When a patient calls and gets an immediate, helpful response — even at 8pm — that's a better experience than any competitor is offering."

## Objection Handling

**"We already have an answering service."**

> Answering services are fine, but they're expensive per call and usually just take a message. They can't actually answer questions about your services, check your schedule, or tell a patient whether you take their insurance. The AI does all of that — and it costs a fraction of what you're paying per call to an answering service.

**"Our patients want to talk to a real person."**

> Totally understand. And for complex situations, the AI routes to your team. But for the calls that are "what time do you open," "do you take Cigna," "I need to reschedule my cleaning" — patients just want a fast answer. The AI gives them that instantly, and your team handles the situations that actually need a human touch.

**"We're locked into our practice management software."**

> The AI receptionist works alongside your existing systems. It's not replacing Dentrix or Eaglesoft — it's catching the calls your team can't get to. Think of it as an extra team member who never takes a break and handles the routine stuff.

**"We need to talk to our IT person / we need to review this with the partners."**

> Absolutely. Here's what I'd suggest — call (928) 325-9472 and let your team hear the AI in action. That usually makes the conversation with partners a lot faster because they can experience it firsthand instead of just reading about it. I'm happy to put together a proposal your team can review.

**"HIPAA concerns."**

> Great question — it shows you're serious about compliance. The AI handles general inquiries and appointment scheduling. It's not accessing patient records, storing PHI, or discussing treatment details. It operates at the same level as your front desk answering a phone call from a new patient — name, contact info, scheduling preference. For anything clinical, it routes to your team.
