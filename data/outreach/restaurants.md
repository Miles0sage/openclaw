> Hey, how's it going? I'm Miles — I run a small tech company here in Flagstaff called OpenClaw. I actually just built the entire online ordering system for Delhi Palace down the road. Their orders went up because people can just pull up the menu on their phone and order ahead now.
>
> I was walking by and thought — you guys have great food, but I noticed your website could be doing a lot more for you. I'm not trying to sell you anything today. I just wanted to introduce myself and see if you'd be open to a quick 10-minute chat sometime this week about how I could help you get more online orders without changing anything about how you run your kitchen.
>
> Here's my card. No pressure at all — but if you want, I can show you exactly what I built for Delhi Palace. It's pretty cool.

**If they ask "how much?":**

> Honestly, it depends on what you need. But I'm local, I'm not some agency charging $10K. I work with small businesses here in Flagstaff and I keep it affordable. Happy to put together a quick quote after we chat.

**If they say "we already have a website":**

> That's great! A lot of places do. The difference is whether your website is actually bringing in orders or just sitting there. Delhi Palace had a site before too — but now their website actually takes orders, shows up on Google when people search "Indian food Flagstaff," and feeds straight into their kitchen. That's the kind of upgrade I'm talking about.

## Key Pain Points to Hit in Conversation

1. **No online ordering (or using third-party apps):** "DoorDash and Grubhub take 25-30% of every order. Your own system means you keep all of it."
2. **Outdated website:** "When someone Googles 'restaurants in Flagstaff,' your website is the first impression. If it looks outdated or doesn't have your menu, they're going to the next result."
3. **Missing Google traffic:** "There are hundreds of people searching for food in Flagstaff every day. If your site isn't optimized, those customers are going to chains or competitors who show up first."
4. **Phone order mistakes:** "Phone orders lead to mistakes — wrong items, wrong quantities. Online ordering means the customer types it in themselves, exactly how they want it."
5. **Can't compete with chains:** "Big chains have apps, loyalty programs, online ordering. You don't need all that — but you do need a website that works as hard as you do."

## Objection Handling

**"We're too busy for this."**

> That's actually the best reason to do it. The whole point is to save you time — fewer phone calls, fewer order mistakes, customers help themselves. Delhi Palace's kitchen runs smoother now because everything comes in digitally instead of over the phone.

**"We tried a website before and it didn't help."**

> Most restaurant websites are just digital brochures — they sit there and don't do anything. What I build is a tool that actually brings in revenue. Online ordering, Google optimization, the works. It's not the same thing.

**"We can't afford it."**

> I get it — margins are tight in this business. That's why I price things for local businesses, not Fortune 500 companies. And the online ordering system usually pays for itself within the first couple months because you're not giving 30% to delivery apps anymore.

**"We use DoorDash/Grubhub."**

> Those are great for visibility, and I wouldn't tell you to drop them. But every order through your own website is an order where you keep 100% of the revenue instead of 70%. Even if you shift 20% of your delivery orders to your own site, that adds up fast.
