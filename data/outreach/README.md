## Which Template to Use When

**Walking into a business in person:** Use the walk-in script. Available for restaurants, barbershops, dental offices, and auto shops. Real estate agents are better reached via email or DM.

**Cold emailing from a list:** Every vertical has an email template. Restaurants and barbershops lean on the Delhi Palace and Surgeon Cuts case studies. Dental and auto focus on the missed-call problem. Real estate focuses on speed-to-lead and after-hours coverage.

**Sliding into DMs:** DM templates are available for restaurants, barbershops, and real estate. These are shorter, more casual, and designed for Instagram or Facebook. Dental and auto are better reached in person or via email — their social media is less conversational.

**Following up:** Every vertical has at least one follow-up email. Dental and real estate have a second follow-up for a final touch. Send the first follow-up 3 days after initial contact. Send the second (if applicable) 7 days after the first follow-up. After that, move on.

## Walk-In Tips

1. **Look like a professional, not a salesperson.** Casual but put-together. You're a local tech founder, not a door-to-door guy.
2. **Ask for the owner or manager by name if you know it.** Google the business beforehand and check their website/Facebook for names.
3. **If they're busy, leave a card and come back.** Don't overstay your welcome. The first visit is just an introduction.
4. **Lead with the case study, not the product.** "I built this for a business down the road" is 10x more compelling than "I sell AI phone systems."
5. **Read the room.** If the owner is stressed or slammed, keep it to 15 seconds: "Hey, I'm Miles, I do AI for local businesses, here's my card, I'll come back when it's less crazy." Respect wins more deals than persistence.
6. **Bring your phone.** Be ready to pull up Delhi Palace's website or call the Surgeon Cuts AI number as a live demo. Showing beats telling.
7. **Don't trash their current setup.** If they have a website, say "that's great." If they use Square, say "solid tool." Then explain what you add on top.

## Email Tips

1. **Subject lines matter more than the body.** Test different ones. The ones in each template are starting points.
2. **Keep it short.** Nobody reads long cold emails. The templates are already trimmed — don't add to them.
3. **One CTA per email.** "Would you have 10 minutes this week?" — that's it. Don't ask them to call the demo AND visit a website AND reply AND schedule a meeting.
4. **Send Tuesday-Thursday, 9-11am.** Avoid Mondays (inbox chaos) and Fridays (mentally checked out).
5. **Personalize the first line.** Swap [BUSINESS_NAME] and [OWNER_NAME] obviously, but also add something specific: "I saw your Google reviews — 4.8 stars is impressive" or "Your Instagram posts look great."

## Follow-Up Cadence

| Touch | Timing  | Channel                                                     |
| ----- | ------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | Day 0   | Walk-in, email, or DM                                       |
| 2     | Day 3   | Follow-up email                                             |
| 3     | Day 10  | Second follow-up (dental/real estate) or final DM           |
| 4     | Day 30+ | Circle back if you're in the area — "Hey, just checking in" |

**Rule: Three touches max, then move on.** If they're not interested after three contacts, they're not a fit right now. Put them in a "circle back in 3 months" list.

## Pricing Guidance (When They Ask)

Don't quote specific prices in cold outreach. Use these talking points:

- "I'm a local guy, not a big agency. I price it for small businesses."
- "It depends on what you need — I can put together a quick quote after we chat."
- "The system usually pays for itself within the first couple months."
- "I'm not here to sell you something you don't need. Let me show you what's possible and we'll figure out what makes sense."

Save pricing discussions for the actual meeting/call when you can understand their needs and frame the investment against their specific revenue numbers.
