## Key Pain Points to Hit in Conversation

1. **Can't answer the phone with greasy hands:** "Your techs can't stop mid-repair to grab the phone. That's not a staffing problem — that's an environment problem. The AI solves it without hiring anyone."
2. **Losing customers to chains:** "Jiffy Lube and Firestone have call centers with 20 people answering phones. You don't need 20 people — you just need an AI that picks up every time."
3. **No online scheduling:** "People want to book an oil change the same way they book a dinner reservation — online, on their phone, without calling. If you can't offer that, you're losing the younger crowd entirely."
4. **Voicemail is a dead end:** "How many people actually leave voicemails? Maybe 1 in 5. The other 4 just call the next shop on Google. The AI eliminates voicemail entirely."
5. **Front desk overhead:** "Hiring someone to sit at the front desk and answer phones costs $15-20/hr. The AI does that job 24/7 for a fraction of the cost, and it never calls in sick."
6. **Weekend and after-hours inquiries:** "Someone Googles 'oil change Flagstaff' on Saturday night and calls your number. Voicemail? They'll forget by Monday. AI answers? They've got an appointment booked before they go to bed."

## Objection Handling

**"We're a small shop, we don't need that."**

> Small shops actually benefit the most. Big chains have entire customer service departments. You've got 2-3 guys turning wrenches. The AI levels that playing field — you get the same always-available phone experience as a dealership, without the overhead.

**"Our customers know to just come in."**

> Your regulars do, for sure. But what about the new customers — the person who just moved to Flagstaff, the tourist with a check engine light, the college student looking for a cheap oil change? Those people call first. If nobody answers, they move on.

**"I don't trust AI with my customers."**

> Fair concern. The AI handles the simple stuff — hours, pricing, scheduling. If someone has a complex issue or an emergency, it routes them to a real person. You're not replacing yourself — you're catching the calls you're already missing.

**"What about estimates? Every job is different."**

> The AI can give base prices for standard services — oil change, tire rotation, inspections, brake pads. For anything custom, it tells the caller exactly that: "Every vehicle is a little different, but let me get you scheduled for a free estimate." It sets expectations and books the appointment. That's the goal.

**"My website is fine."**

> If your website has your services, pricing, and a way to schedule online — you're ahead of most shops. But if it's just a Google listing with your phone number, and that phone isn't being answered, then your entire customer funnel has a hole in it. The AI plugs that hole.
